# Reinventing Your Dealership with AI and Omnichannel Journeys
In this session, presented by Faraaz Ghauri, Vice President of Digital Retail, and Eva Kellershof, Vice President of Sales for North America and Europe at NETSOL Technologies, in partnership with the Greater New York Automobile Dealers Association, NETSOL shares how forward-thinking dealers are using AI and omnichannel strategies to reinvent their [automotive digital retail solutions](https://netsoltech.com/products/digital-retail), from lead generation through to F&I, to deliver a unified retail experience that boosts efficiency, builds trust, and drives measurable improvements in profitability.
## About this webinar
The automotive retail landscape has shifted faster in the last three years than in the previous two decades. Customers now begin 70% of their buying journey online, yet most dealerships still treat digital and in-store as disconnected experiences, forcing customers to restart conversations, re-enter information, and re-negotiate terms when they walk through the showroom door. The dealerships that are closing that gap are those combining AI-powered decisioning with a true omnichannel infrastructure, where every touchpoint shares the same data, the same deal state, and the same customer view.
For New York area dealerships, where competition is dense and customer expectations are high, this is not an incremental improvement, it is a structural reinvention. This session examines what that reinvention looks like in practice, specifically around [vehicle finance software](https://netsoltech.com/solutions/automotive-finance) workflows, personalised F&I experiences, and AI-driven engagement that increases conversion without adding operational complexity.
## What you'll learn
This session is built for dealership principals, general managers, and digital retail leads evaluating how to apply AI and omnichannel strategy to drive real business outcomes. It covers:
  * Why traditional dealership sales processes are losing customers and what the reinvented model looks like end to end.
  * How AI-powered tools such as virtual assistants, predictive lead scoring, and personalized F&I recommendations are being deployed in live dealership environments today.
  * What a true omnichannel journey means in practice: consistent pricing, deal state, and customer data across every channel, without requiring a full technology overhaul.
  * The specific dealership metrics that AI and omnichannel investment moves: lead-to-sale conversion, gross per unit, F&I attachment rates, and customer retention.
  * A practical roadmap for introducing these capabilities into an existing dealership operation without disrupting the workflows your teams already know.


## Key themes from the discussion
Three strategic themes ran through the session:
  * **The customer journey must be continuous, not channel-specific:** Customers who start online and finish in-store are the majority, and they expect the transition to be invisible. When trade-in values, payment calculations, and credit decisions carry across from the digital experience into the showroom without restart, deal cycle times drop and customer satisfaction scores rise. The blog [winning iin auto retail](https://netsoltech.com/blog/winning-the-future-of-auto-retail) covers how AI and integrated technology are delivering exactly these outcomes for dealerships operating across multiple brands and locations.
  * **AI works best when it is embedded in the deal, not bolted onto the side of it:** The most productive AI deployments discussed in this session are those that support the deal flow directly, surfacing the right F&I product for a specific customer profile, scheduling follow-ups based on buyer behaviour signals, and guiding customers through financing options in real time. These are not experimental features; they are live in dealership environments delivering measurable uplifts in gross per unit and F&I attachment rates.
  * **Reinvention does not require replacement:** One of the key practical points from the session is that dealerships do not need to rip and replace existing DMS or CRM infrastructure to build an omnichannel capability. A modular, API-first platform can connect existing systems and add AI-powered layers on top, delivering the unified experience customers expect without the disruption a full system change would cause.
