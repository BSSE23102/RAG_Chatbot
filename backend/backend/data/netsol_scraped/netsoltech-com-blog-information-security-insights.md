## **The unseen threats: What are companies missing?**
Despite significant investments in cybersecurity policies and procedures, many organizations fail to address key vulnerabilities. A McKinsey report reveals that 60% of companies are not adequately protected against insider threats. This gap in security can have devastating consequences, as insider attacks often go undetected until it's too late. The average cost of a data breach in 2023 was $4.35 million, according to IBM's Cost of a Data Breach Report. This staggering figure underscores the need for comprehensive security strategies and best practices that cover all potential threat vectors.
The average cost of a data breach in 2023 was $4.35 million.
- IBM
### **Phishing and social engineering: The human element**
Phishing attacks are on the rise, with [Statista](https://www.statista.com/study/165487/phishing-in-2023/) reporting a 13% increase in phishing incidents in 2023. These attacks exploit human vulnerabilities, making it essential for organizations to invest in employee training and awareness programs. A leading financial institution implemented a rigorous phishing simulation program, reducing successful phishing attempts by 70% within a year. This proactive approach highlights the importance of equipping employees with the knowledge to identify and respond to phishing attempts.
### **Advanced persistent threats (APTs): The silent invaders**
Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs) are stealthy attacks that can remain undetected for extended periods, causing significant damage. Many organizations are unaware of the sophisticated techniques used by APTs, such as zero-day exploits and spear-phishing. Companies must adopt advanced detection and response solutions, such as endpoint detection and response (EDR) and network traffic analysis (NTA). These tools can help identify unusual activity and respond to threats before they escalate.
### **The importance of regular security audits**
Regular security audits are crucial for identifying and addressing vulnerabilities. A study by Ponemon Institute found that organizations conducting frequent security audits were 35% less likely to experience a data breach. Did you know? Only 40% of companies conduct annual [cyber security services](https://netsoltech.com/blog/changing-financial-trends-and-the-threat-to-cybersecurity), leaving them exposed to potential threats. Implementing regular audits can help organizations stay ahead of emerging threats and ensure compliance with industry regulations.
### **Cloud security: The new frontier**
With the rapid adoption of cloud services, ensuring cloud security has become a top priority. However, a [Gartner report](https://www.gartner.com/smarterwithgartner/is-the-cloud-secure) indicates that by 2025, 99% of cloud security failures will be the customer's fault. This statistic highlights the need for robust cloud security policies and practices.
Use Case: A multinational corporation faced a major data breach due to misconfigured cloud storage. By partnering with a cloud security provider, they implemented automated configuration checks and continuous monitoring, significantly reducing their risk of future breaches.
By 2025, 99% of cloud security failures will be the customer's fault.

### **The role of Artificial Intelligence in Infosec**
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is revolutionizing the field of information security. AI-powered tools can analyze vast amounts of data to detect anomalies and predict potential threats. According to Statista, the global market for AI in cybersecurity is expected to reach $38.2 billion by 2026. What needs to be done? Organizations should leverage AI to enhance their security capabilities. Implementing AI-driven threat intelligence and automated response systems can provide a significant advantage in the fight against cyber threats.
